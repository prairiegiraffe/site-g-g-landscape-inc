Landscaping for Sundance and Crook County

G&G Landscape provides full-service landscaping to Sundance, the Crook County seat, in northeast Wyoming's Black Hills region. The terrain here is different from the open plains to the west — rolling hills, ponderosa pine, and rocky ground create both opportunities and challenges for landscape work. We plan every project around the specific conditions of the site, not a one-size-fits-all template.

Sundance sits near the gateway to Devils Tower and close to the South Dakota border, surrounded by ranch properties and small-town residential lots. Whether a project calls for retaining walls on a hillside, irrigation for a commercial property along the I-90 corridor, or erosion control on a rural parcel, we bring the right equipment and crew from our Gillette operations.

The Black Hills foothills mean more tree cover, different soil profiles, and steeper grades than other parts of our service area. We adapt our approach — selecting plant material that handles the elevation, designing drainage systems for the terrain, and building hardscapes that work with the natural landscape rather than against it.
